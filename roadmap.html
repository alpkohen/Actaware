Product roadmap

How we’re thinking about official UK source coverage and why we no longer use “zero compliance blind spots” on the pricing page.

Scope today

ActAware monitors 17 official UK feeds (GOV.UK, HMRC, Home Office, ICO, EHRC, Acas, TPR, HSE, Legislation.gov.uk, Employment Tribunal decisions, DWP employer-facing content, and related channels listed on our homepage). We summarise changes as plain-English, employer-focused actions — information only, not legal advice.

We do not claim to capture every compliance obligation in the UK. Our disclaimer states we do not guarantee completeness.

Why we changed the headline

Independent review noted real gaps — for example Employment Appeal Tribunal (EAT) binding precedent and some sector regulators. “Zero blind spots” overpromised. The pricing section now says daily clarity on core UK employer compliance, and this page sets out what we plan to add next.

Planned additions (priority)

  1. EAT (Employment Appeal Tribunal) — highest priority. ET first-instance decisions aren’t binding precedent; EAT and higher courts matter for many principles. Likely via BAILII-style ingestion after legal/ToS review and when core product + billing are stable. May be Professional/Agency or high-importance flagged items first.
  2. DBS — medium. Relevant for health, education, and care employers; fits sector-tailored positioning.
  3. CQC (and similar sector bodies) — medium for Agency/advisors serving social care clients; phased by sector to avoid scope creep.
  4. CAC / Certification Officer — lower priority unless customer demand for union/recognition topics is clear.
  5. Scotland / Wales — lower; much employment law is reserved, but some nuance exists for apprenticeships and public-sector angles.
  6. GLAA → FWA — we already monitor the Fair Work Agency; we’ll align as GLAA functions transfer per government timeline.

Out of scope (for now)

FCA / SM&CR and similar financial-conduct regimes are adjacent but not the same as general employer compliance; a separate product line would be needed if we ever offered them.

How we prioritise

  • Customer feedback and support themes
  • Cost of ingestion + AI vs. clear employer value
  • Legal clarity on using each official feed
